# Life-expectancy-project
This project analyzes life expectancy data from 2000 to 2015 for 193 countries, using data from the World Health Organization (WHO). The goal is to identify and understand the key factors that influence life expectancy, such as GDP, healthcare spending, and population size. 

Problem Statement:-
Life expectancy varies widely across different countries and regions, influenced by a range of factors like economic development, healthcare spending, and population size. Understanding what drives these differences can help identify areas for improvement in public health. The problem is to explore the factors that impact life expectancy, analyze trends from 2000 to 2015, and provide clear insights into how these factors influence the longevity of populations worldwide.

Key Insights:-
1.	Wealthier countries live longer: There’s a clear link between GDP and life expectancy. Richer countries tend to have better healthcare and higher living standards, which lead to longer life expectancy.
2.	Health spending matters: Countries that spend more on healthcare tend to have higher life expectancy, showing that good healthcare systems make a big difference.
3.	Regional differences: Life expectancy is much higher in regions like North America and Europe compared to Africa and parts of Asia. This highlights the gap in healthcare and living conditions around the world.
4.	Population size impacts life expectancy: Larger countries often have lower average life expectancy, due to disparities in healthcare access, despite their economic growth.
